Sidheshwar Temple

It is sacred to Hindus, especially the members of the Lingayat denomination.The temple is dedicated to its namesake Siddheshwar, a twelfth-century devotee of Shiva who is considered a god in the Lingayat faith and the grāmadevatā of Solapur. It is at the temple's site where he is recorded as having attained samadhi, and as such the location is revered by devotees. Upon achieving samadhi in 1167AD, Siddeshwar attained samadhi and A marble samadhi exists at the centre of the temple today.






Swami Samarth Temple

The Swami Samarth Temple in Akkalkot, Solapur, is a significant pilgrimage site dedicated to Swami Samarth Maharaj, a 19th-century saint believed to be an incarnation of Lord Dattatreya. The temple is located in Akkalkot, about 40 km from Solapur city. Devotees visit this temple to seek blessings, solace, and guidance from Swami Samarth.








Bhuikot Fort

Bhuikot Killa, also known as Solapur Fort, is a historical land fort located in Solapur, Maharashtra, India. It was built during the Bahmani Sultanate era in the 14th century. The fort is known for its unique construction and has witnessed various historical events, including being under the control of the Mughals, Marathas, and the British. Today, it houses an animal park and a garden, making it a popular tourist spot.







Vitthal Temple

The Vitthal Temple in Pandharpur, near Solapur, is a major Hindu pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Vitthal, a form of Vishnu, and his consort Rukmini (Rakhumai). It's known for its unique architectural style and is a center of the Varkari tradition. The temple is situated on the banks of the Chandrabhaga River, and a dip in the river is considered sacred.










Iskcon Temple

ISKCON Solapur temple is part of the global network of ISKCON temples dedicated to the teachings of Lord Krishna. The temple is a spiritual haven for devotees and visitors, offering a serene environment for worship and meditation.It features beautiful deities of Radha and Krishna, and regularly hosts religious ceremonies, festivals, and cultural programs.
