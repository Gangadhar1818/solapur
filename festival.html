Ganesh Festival

The Ganesh festival in Solapur has a rich history, with the public celebration of the festival reportedly starting in 1885 with the Ajoba Ganpati in Shukrawar Peth. This predates the more widely known public celebration in Pune by Lokmanya Tilak, who was reportedly inspired by the Solapur celebrations. Ajoba Ganpati is now in its 140th year and is nearing the 150-year milestone.The Ajoba Ganpati, located in Manik Chowk, has a grand temple and is a focal point of the celebrations in Solapur.






Sidheshwar Yatra

The Siddheshwar festival in Solapur, also known as Siddheshwar Yatra, is a 15-day festival dedicated to Lord Siddharama, held at the Shri Siddheshwar Temple. It starts on Shakambari Poornima (the full moon in January) and ends on Amavasya (the no-moon day). The festival is renowned for the Gadda Yatra, a grand procession featuring decorated bamboo sticks symbolizing a bride and groom, and attracts a large number of devotees.






Shiv Jayanti

In 2025, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Jayanti will be celebrated on February 19th, which falls on a Wednesday. This day is a public holiday in Maharashtra and is celebrated with much enthusiasm and various events in Solapur. In Solapur, you can expect to see rallies, parades, and cultural programs organized to commemorate the occasion.









Holi

Holika Dahan, also known as Choti Holi, is the celebration that precedes the main Holi festival and involves the burning of a bonfire to symbolize the victory of good over evil. In Solapur, as in other parts of India, Holika Dahan is observed on the full moon night of the Hindu month of Phalgun (usually in March). The bonfire represents the burning of Holika, who was consumed by the flames while attempting to harm her nephew Prahlad.








Diwali

Diwali in Solapur is a vibrant celebration focused on the traditional Lakshmi Puja, along with festive decorations and faral (sweets and snacks). Drik Panchang says Lakshmi Puja will be on Monday, October 20, 2025, during the Pradosh Kaal. Solapur is also known for its Diwali editions of local newspapers, which are a significant part of the cultural landscape.
