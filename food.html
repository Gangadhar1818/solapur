शेंगा चटणी

Shenga chutney, also known as peanut or shengdana chutney, is a popular and authentic condiment from Solapur, Maharashtra. It's a spicy, nutty, and flavorful chutney made from roasted peanuts, red chilies, garlic, and cumin. This traditional Maharashtrian recipe is a staple in many households and is enjoyed with various dishes like bhakri, roti, rice, and snacks.







Vada Pav

Solapur is known for its vada pav, with several popular spots serving the iconic Indian street food. Solapur Vada Pav at Kumta Naka and Kaka Vada Pav are well-regarded, according to restaurant directories. Other notable places include Supriya Snacks Jumbo Wada Pav, known for its larger-than-usual vada pav size.









Paper Dosa

Solapur paper dosa refers to the popular South Indian-style paper dosa served at various eateries in Solapur, Maharashtra. This dish is known for its ultra-thin, crispy texture, achieved by spreading a well-fermented batter of idli rice and urad dal (split black lentils) into a large, thin circle on a hot tawa (griddle). The batter often includes a small amount of fenugreek seeds for flavor and fermentation, and is sometimes kick-started with a bit of yogurt





Dal chaval

Solapuri Dalchaval (also known as Solapuri Dalcha Rice or Solapur Dal Chawal) is a highly popular and iconic dish from Solapur, Maharashtra. Dalcha is a rich, spicy lentil-based curry, often made with a mix of lentils like lal masoor (red lentils) and moong dal, along with vegetables such as bottle gourd. In non-vegetarian versions, mutton is commonly added, but vegetarian versions are also widely enjoyed







Puri Bhaji

Solapuri Puri Bhaji is a beloved breakfast and snack dish from Solapur, Maharashtra, featuring two main components: puri (deep-fried wheat bread) and bhaji (spiced potato curry). While the basic concept is popular across India, Solapuri Puri Bhaji is known for its regional spice profile and hearty flavors.Made from wheat flour, sometimes with a bit of semolina (rava) for crispness, salt, sugar, and oil. The dough is rolled into small discs and deep-fried until puffed and golden
